## Resolution comparison

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="SD (Standard Definition)" icon="mobile-screen">
    **Resolution:** 480p (854 x 480 pixels)
    
    Standard Definition delivers your custom intro at a web-friendly size that loads quickly and plays smoothly on any device.
    
    ### When to choose SD
    - Sharing on social media platforms
    - Embedding in websites or blogs
    - Sending via messaging apps
    - Mobile-first viewing
    - Quick downloads needed
    - Storage space is limited
    
    ### Quality characteristics
    - Clear and watchable on small to medium screens
    - Smaller file size for faster delivery
    - Smooth playback even on slower connections
    - The iconic Stranger Things aesthetic is preserved
    
    <Tip>
      SD is perfect for Instagram stories, TikTok, or any platform where users primarily view content on mobile devices.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="HD (High Definition)" icon="desktop">
    **Resolution:** 1080p (1920 x 1080 pixels)
    
    High Definition provides excellent quality for modern displays and is the most popular choice for general use.
    
    ### When to choose HD
    - YouTube videos
    - Desktop or laptop viewing
    - TV screen presentations
    - Most video editing projects
    - Balancing quality and file size
    - Standard professional work
    
    ### Quality characteristics
    - Crystal clear on HD monitors and TVs
    - Sharp text rendering of your custom titles
    - Rich detail in the signature red glow effects
    - Excellent color reproduction
    - Manageable file size
    
    <Note>
      HD (1080p) is the sweet spot for most users. It provides excellent quality while keeping file sizes reasonable for easy sharing and storage.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="4K (Ultra High Definition)" icon="tv">
    **Resolution:** 4K/2160p (3840 x 2160 pixels)
    
    Ultra High Definition offers the absolute highest quality output with stunning detail and clarity.
    
    ### When to choose 4K
    - Professional video production
    - Large screen displays or projectors
    - Future-proofing your content
    - Cinematic projects
    - High-end marketing materials
    - Archival purposes
    - Maximum editing flexibility
    
    ### Quality characteristics
    - Exceptional detail and sharpness
    - Perfect for large 4K TVs and monitors
    - Maximum color depth and fidelity
    - Pristine text rendering
    - Professional-grade output
    - Allows for cropping or zooming in post-production
    
    <Warning>
      4K files are significantly larger than HD or SD. Ensure you have adequate storage space and bandwidth for downloading and storing your video.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## File size and quality considerations

Understanding the trade-offs between resolution, file size, and quality helps you make the right choice:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="File sizes">
    Higher resolutions produce larger file sizes:
    
    - **SD:** Smallest files, fastest to download and share
    - **HD:** Medium-sized files, balance of quality and convenience
    - **4K:** Largest files, requires more storage and bandwidth
    
    <Note>
      Consider your storage space and how you plan to share the video when choosing a resolution.
    </Note>
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Visual quality">
    How each resolution looks on different displays:
    
    **SD (480p):**
    - Excellent: Small screens (phones)
    - Good: Tablets
    - Acceptable: Laptops
    - Pixelated: Large TVs and monitors
    
    **HD (1080p):**
    - Excellent: Phones, tablets, laptops
    - Excellent: 1080p TVs and monitors
    - Good: 4K displays (upscaled)
    - Perfect: Most modern devices
    
    **4K (2160p):**
    - Excellent: All screens
    - Native quality: 4K TVs and monitors
    - Future-proof: Upcoming display technologies
    - Overkill: Phones and small tablets (but can be downscaled)
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Use case matrix">
    Quick reference for choosing the right resolution:
    
    | Your Goal | Recommended Resolution |
    |-----------|------------------------|
    | Social media post | SD |
    | YouTube video | HD |
    | Professional portfolio | 4K |
    | Email attachment | SD |
    | Website embed | SD or HD |
    | TV broadcast | HD or 4K |
    | Film festival submission | 4K |
    | Quick preview/test | SD |
    | Wedding video intro | HD or 4K |
    | School project | HD |
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Technical specifications

Your video will be delivered in a standard video format compatible with most devices and platforms:

- **Format:** MP4 video file
- **Aspect Ratio:** Widescreen (16:9)
- **Audio:** Includes the iconic Stranger Things theme music

<Tip>
  MP4 files work on virtually all devices and can be easily shared on social media, uploaded to YouTube, or used in video editing software.
</Tip>

## Making your choice

Still not sure which resolution to choose? Consider these questions:

1. **Where will you primarily display this intro?**
   - Mobile/social media → SD
   - Computer/YouTube → HD
   - TV/professional use → 4K

2. **Do you need to edit the video further?**
   - No editing planned → Any resolution
   - Basic editing → HD
   - Professional editing with effects → 4K

3. **How will you share it?**
   - Email attachment → SD
   - Cloud link/YouTube → HD or 4K
   - Embed on website → SD or HD

4. **What's your storage situation?**
   - Limited space → SD
   - Average storage → HD
   - Plenty of space → 4K

<Warning>
  You cannot change the resolution after your intro is generated. Choose carefully based on your intended use.
</Warning>
